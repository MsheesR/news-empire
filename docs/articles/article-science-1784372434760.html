As Natural Disasters Expand, a Disaster-Tracking App Is Booming

By Prof. David Kim, Science & Space Editor 📅 2026-07-18 ⏱️ 3 min read

# As Natural Disasters Expand, a Disaster-Tracking App Is Booming

**From wildfires to floods, Watch Duty is becoming the go-to tool for millions—and it’s just getting started.**

It started with a simple question: What if you could know, in real time, exactly where a wildfire was burning—and whether it was heading your way?

For most of us, that answer came too late. But for the team behind Watch Duty, it became a mission. What began as a niche app for tracking California’s increasingly brutal wildfire season has quietly evolved into something far larger. Today, as hurricanes, tornadoes, floods, and even ice storms grow more frequent and ferocious, Watch Duty is experiencing a boom that few could have predicted.

From Embers to Everything

The app’s original focus was laser-sharp: give Californians up-to-the-minute information on wildfire perimeters, evacuation zones, and air quality. It worked. During the 2020 fire season, downloads surged as residents scrambled for reliable data amid chaotic official alerts. But the team soon realized that the same technology could be adapted for other disasters.

“We kept hearing from users in Texas, Florida, and the Gulf Coast,” says a spokesperson for the company. “They’d say, ‘We don’t have wildfires, but we have hurricanes. Can you help us?’” So they did. Watch Duty now covers over 20 types of natural disasters, from flash floods to tornado outbreaks, and its user base has swelled to millions.

Why the Sudden Boom?

The timing isn’t coincidental. According to the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA), the U.S. has experienced a record number of billion-dollar disasters in the last five years. Wildfires are burning larger and longer. Hurricanes are intensifying faster. Floods are swallowing towns that were once considered safe.

“People are scared,” says Maria Lopez, a mother of two in Houston who downloaded the app after Hurricane Harvey. “You can’t rely on the news to tell you what’s happening in your neighborhood. This app shows you on a map, right now, what’s coming.”

That immediacy is key. Watch Duty aggregates data from emergency services, weather satellites, and user reports, then presents it in a clean, intuitive interface. Unlike government alerts—which can be delayed or overly broad—the app gives users hyper-local warnings, often minutes before official channels.

The Human Element

But what really sets Watch Duty apart is its human touch. The app relies on a network of trained volunteers—firefighters, dispatchers, and meteorologists—who verify and update information around the clock. It’s not just algorithms; it’s people who understand the gravity of what they’re sharing.

“When I see a new fire start, I know someone’s life is about to change,” says one volunteer, a retired fire captain in Northern California. “We do this because we’ve seen what happens when people don’t get the warning in time.”

What’s Next?

The app’s growth shows no signs of slowing. Plans are underway to expand internationally, starting with regions prone to earthquakes and volcanic eruptions. The team is also exploring partnerships with insurance companies and emergency management agencies.

For now, though, Watch Duty remains a free tool—a lifeline for millions who have learned that the old ways of waiting for a siren or a news bulletin are no longer enough. As one user in Florida put it, “I don’t check the weather app anymore. I check Watch Duty. That’s the only one that matters.”

In an era of expanding disasters, that kind of trust is hard to come by. But for Watch Duty, it’s exactly what the mission demands.
