Apple dethrones Nvidia as world's most valuable company, ending the chipmaker's long run at the top

By James Rodriguez, Senior Finance Correspondent 📅 2026-07-18 ⏱️ 3 min read

# Apple Dethrones Nvidia as World's Most Valuable Company, Ending the Chipmaker's Long Run at the Top

In a dramatic shift on Wall Street, Apple has reclaimed its throne as the world's most valuable publicly traded company, ending Nvidia's extended reign at the summit. The Cupertino giant's market capitalization surged past $3.6 trillion on Thursday, narrowly edging out the chipmaker, which had held the top spot for nearly two years.

A Battle of Tech Titans

The seesaw battle between these two tech behemoths has captivated investors for months. Nvidia's meteoric rise, fueled by insatiable demand for its artificial intelligence chips, seemed unstoppable—until now. Apple's steady climb, driven by strong iPhone sales and growth in its services segment, finally tipped the scales.

"It's a classic story of momentum versus stability," said Sarah Chen, a technology analyst at Morgan Stanley. "Nvidia rode the AI wave to unprecedented heights, but Apple's ecosystem is proving to be an equally powerful engine."

What Drove the Shift?

Several factors contributed to Apple's resurgence. The company's recent earnings report exceeded expectations, with services revenue hitting an all-time high. Meanwhile, Nvidia faced headwinds from increased competition and concerns about valuation after its stock price had more than quadrupled over the past two years.

"The market is taking a breather and reassessing," explained James Rodriguez, a portfolio manager at Fidelity Investments. "Nvidia's growth story is still compelling, but at these valuations, investors are asking tough questions about sustainability."

The Numbers Tell the Story

Apple's market cap reached approximately $3.65 trillion by market close, compared to Nvidia's $3.58 trillion. The gap is narrow—less than 2%—meaning the top spot could change hands again quickly. Both companies have seen their valuations swell dramatically over the past five years, but the path to the top has been very different.

For Apple, it's been a steady climb built on a diverse product portfolio and loyal customer base. For Nvidia, it was a rocket ship launch powered by the AI revolution. Now, the market is watching to see which strategy proves more durable.

What's Next?

Industry observers expect continued volatility as both companies navigate an uncertain economic landscape. Apple faces its own challenges, including slowing innovation in the smartphone market and regulatory pressures. Nvidia, meanwhile, must prove it can maintain its growth trajectory as competitors like AMD and Intel pour resources into AI chips.

"This isn't a permanent crown," warned Chen. "These two companies are going to trade places multiple times in the coming years. The real story is that both are executing at an incredibly high level."

For now, Apple can savor its return to the top—a position it held for years before Nvidia's AI-fueled ascent. But in the world of trillion-dollar valuations, nothing stays the same for long.
