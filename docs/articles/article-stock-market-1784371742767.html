If Valar Atomics is worth $6B, $OKLO is an absolute steal

By James Rodriguez, Senior Finance Correspondent 📅 2026-07-18 ⏱️ 3 min read

**Headline:** If Valar Atomics is Worth $6 Billion, $OKLO is an Absolute Steal

**By [Staff Writer]**

The nuclear energy startup scene is heating up, and a recent valuation in the private markets has Wall Street analysts and retail investors alike scratching their heads. Valar Atomics, a privately held advanced nuclear reactor company, is reportedly in talks to raise a new round of funding at a staggering **$6 billion valuation**. That figure alone is enough to turn heads, but when you place it next to its publicly traded competitor, Oklo Inc. (NYSE: $OKLO), the numbers start to look a little strange.

Here’s the kicker: Oklo currently trades at a market capitalization of roughly **$7 billion**. For those keeping score at home, that means the public market is valuing Oklo only marginally higher than an early-stage private startup that has yet to achieve commercial scale.

# The Case for Oklo

If you strip away the hype and look at the fundamentals, the gap in valuation between these two companies seems almost impossible to justify. Oklo isn’t just a concept on paper; it’s a company that has been grinding through the regulatory maze for years. The company boasts a significantly more mature regulatory position than Valar, having worked extensively with the Nuclear Regulatory Commission (NRC) to advance its Aurora design.

But the real differentiator is the pipeline. Oklo currently commands an **18 GW commercial pipeline**, which includes signed long-term power agreements. Perhaps most notably, the company has a **1.2 GW binding agreement with Meta**—yes, that Meta—to supply clean, reliable power for data centers. That’s not a handshake or a letter of intent; that’s a binding deal.

Beyond that, Oklo has racked up multiple Department of Energy (DOE) awards and strategic partnerships, giving it a level of institutional validation that most startups can only dream of. The company is also building out a growing fuel recycling and radioisotope business, diversifying its revenue streams beyond just power generation. And let’s not forget the balance sheet: Oklo is sitting on billions of dollars in cash to execute its roadmap.

# What Valar Has Done

None of this is to diminish what Valar Atomics has achieved. The company deserves credit for a significant milestone: its recent successful demonstration of the Ward 250 reactor. Valar managed to generate electricity from an advanced reactor and used it to power an NVIDIA AI system. That’s a real technical achievement.

But context is everything. The Ward 250 is an early, small-scale proof-of-concept. It’s a demonstration, not a commercial deployment capable of supplying meaningful power to the massive data centers that the AI boom demands. Valar is still years away from having a product that can actually plug into the grid and serve a customer like Meta or Amazon.

# The Valuation Gap

So why is the market treating these two companies as nearly equals? Private investors are assigning Valar a ~$6 billion valuation for a promising prototype. Meanwhile, Oklo—which has years of regulatory work, a massive signed pipeline, binding contracts, and billions in cash—is trading at just $7 billion.

The math doesn’t add up. Oklo is entirely focused on commercial deployment. They have spent years advancing the Aurora design through rigorous regulatory milestones and customer development. They have the contracts. They have the cash. They have the roadmap.

If Valar Atomics is worth $6 billion, then Oklo’s current market cap represents an absolute steal. For investors looking at the nuclear renaissance and the insatiable energy demands of AI, the gap between these two companies is an opportunity that is hard to ignore.
