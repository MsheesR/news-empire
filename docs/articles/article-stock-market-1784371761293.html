Chipmakers and other high-flying stocks slide as AI trade wobbles

By James Rodriguez, Senior Finance Correspondent 📅 2026-07-18 ⏱️ 4 min read

# Chipmakers and Other High-Flying Stocks Slide as AI Trade Wobbles

**The great artificial intelligence rally that has powered markets for the better part of two years hit a serious speed bump this week, sending shares of chipmakers and other AI-adjacent stocks into a tailspin.**

Investors who had grown accustomed to watching their portfolios soar on the back of the AI boom suddenly found themselves staring at red screens. The sell-off was broad and brutal, cutting across the entire AI ecosystem—from the semiconductor giants that make the brains of AI systems to the data center operators and software companies that have been riding the wave.

What Triggered the Panic?

The downturn didn't come out of nowhere. A confluence of factors spooked the market, starting with growing concerns that the blistering pace of AI-related spending may be outpacing actual returns. For months, analysts have been warning that the billions being poured into AI infrastructure might not translate into profits as quickly as the market has been pricing in.

"Investors are finally asking the hard questions," said one portfolio manager who requested anonymity because he wasn't authorized to speak publicly. "They want to know when these massive capital expenditures are actually going to show up in the bottom line. And right now, the answers aren't comforting."

The jitters were compounded by a surprise earnings warning from a major Asian chipmaker, which cited softening demand in certain segments. That was all it took to ignite a wave of profit-taking in a market that had become dangerously overextended.

The Magnificent Seven Take a Hit

The so-called "Magnificent Seven" tech stocks—Apple, Microsoft, Alphabet, Amazon, Nvidia, Tesla, and Meta—were not spared. Nvidia, the poster child of the AI revolution and perhaps the most-watched stock on Wall Street, saw its shares tumble more than 4% in a single session. Other chipmakers like AMD and Intel followed suit, with losses ranging from 3% to 6%.

The sell-off wasn't limited to the usual suspects. Smaller companies that had been swept up in the AI frenzy, from obscure data center operators to software firms with a whisper of an AI product, saw their valuations get slashed in half in some cases.

Is This a Correction or Something More?

The big question on everyone's mind is whether this is just a healthy pullback in an overheated market or the beginning of a more serious reckoning for AI stocks.

"I've been through enough cycles to know that when everyone is convinced a technology is going to change the world, that's usually when you need to be most careful," said a veteran market strategist. "AI is real. It's transformative. But that doesn't mean every company with an AI pitch is worth a hundred times earnings."

The volatility has reignited debates about whether the AI trade has become too crowded. Hedge funds and institutional investors have been piling into AI stocks for months, and when everyone is on the same side of the boat, even a small shift in sentiment can cause big waves.

What Comes Next?

For now, the market is in a wait-and-see mode. The upcoming earnings season will be crucial, as investors demand concrete evidence that AI investments are generating real revenue growth. Companies that can't deliver may find themselves punished even more severely.

One thing is certain: the easy money in AI stocks is over. The days of buying any stock with "AI" in its name and watching it double are likely behind us. From here on out, it's going to be about fundamentals, execution, and real-world results.

As one trader put it, "The hangover has finally arrived. Now we'll find out who was just drinking the Kool-Aid and who actually has a real business."
